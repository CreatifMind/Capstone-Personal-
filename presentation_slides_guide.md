## 2. Complete Speaker Script (Section-by-Section)

### Section 1: Cover & Intro (1.0 minute)
> *"Good morning, Dr. Narishah and members of the panel. Today, our group is excited to present PurityLoop AI: an Automated Waste Sorting and Contamination Detection System designed for Malaysian materials recovery facilities. PurityLoop AI is an integrated, dual-layer computer vision and RPA platform. It transforms manual waste sorting into an audited, real-time automated process, increasing recyclable purity rates to 90% and above, while providing a verifiable operational process accountability trail for corporate ESG disclosures. I will now hand over to Naomi to introduce our problem statement and baseline metrics."*

### Section 2: Opening Hook & Business Problem (2.5 minutes)
> *"Let's look at the contamination crisis. In Malaysia, solid waste generation reached 15.2 million tonnes in 2024. Despite national efforts targeting a 40% recycling rate by 2025 under the 12th Malaysia Plan, we achieved only 37.9% in 2024 due to high contamination rates. An estimated 6.96% of potentially recyclable material—nearly one million tonnes—is diverted to landfills annually because it is contaminated at source or poorly sorted. Manual sorting line operators face high cognitive load, repetitive stress injuries, and severe safety hazards. The most volatile danger is lithium-ion batteries; when compressed in mechanical compactors, they undergo thermal runaway, triggering facility-wide fires. Our solution, PurityLoop AI, replaces this manual workflow with an edge camera feed running YOLOv8m-seg instance segmentation. It detects and outlines items in real time, logs telemetry to a local SQLite database, and pushes metrics to a Power BI dashboard and a citizen-facing Streamlit app to drive behavioural change at source."*

### Section 3: Methodology & The Most Challenging Stage (2.5 minutes)
> *"To construct a system that is both operationally robust and scientifically valid, we adopted an Integrated Multi-Framework Approach. The overall lifecycle is governed by an Iterative Waterfall model for milestone checkpoints. We use Lean Six Sigma DMAIC for process quality control, BALC and CRISP-DM to align data mining with business outcomes, and Agile Sprints for our Streamlit UI development. Our daily data science pipeline follows the five stages of OSEMN: Obtain, Scrub, Explore, Model, and iNterpret.*
> 
> *Our most challenging phase was the **Scrub/Data Preparation** stage within OSEMN. We faced severe class imbalance—with standard food waste dominating our 227k master image corpus while the safety-critical battery class was scarce. Furthermore, public datasets contain significant crowdsourced label noise. We overcame these challenges by fusing specialized e-waste collections to amass 12,814 battery images, running Perceptual Hashing to eliminate data leakage across splits, and executing **Cleanlab** audits to algorithmically prune 3,420 noisy annotations. Most importantly, we implemented an **Asymmetric Confidence Gate**: standard materials auto-sort at 85% confidence, but batteries enforce a strict 90% recall threshold. Any battery detection below 90% is held in the Amber Queue for operator verification rather than being allowed to pass into compactors."*

### Section 4: Technical & Analytical Core (5.0 minutes)
> *"Let's dive into the technical core. Our machine learning engine is built on **YOLOv8m-seg**. Unlike simple bounding boxes, instance segmentation provides pixel-level polygon masks, allowing us to estimate object volume and mass even for crumpled or overlapping waste on a fast-moving belt. We select the medium variant to balance processing latency against small-object detection accuracy. Our training workflow consists of two phases: a backbone-frozen reference training run, followed by full fine-tuning with cosine annealing decay. To ensure pipeline inteoperational auditty, we enforce diagnostic quality checks requiring individual training slices to pass a class-specific AP threshold of at least 60% before they are merged.*
> 
> *Our backend utilizes a Python RPA watchdog script. When the edge camera writes a frame, the script validates file inteoperational auditty, calls the YOLO inference pipeline, and commits labels, certainty, and zone classifications to a local SQLite database within 100 milliseconds. This high speed prevents conveyor queue build-ups.*
> 
> *To translate these raw detections into operational value, we use a Material Yield Assessment operational performance auditing formula. The model estimates each item's weight using class constants, subtracts the carbon emission factor of recycled material from virgin material using global Yield standards, and calculates the total Weight saved. Crucially, the total is multiplied by the session's purity rate. A contaminated batch cannot be fully processed, so this purity multiplier ensures that our sustainability claims are completely audit-ready and compliant with operational audit standards."*

### Section 5: Prototype Demo & Contribution (2.0 minutes)
> *"We have built a fully functional double-portal Streamlit prototype. On the right, you see the **Internal Operations Screen** designed for facility supervisors and managers. It displays live conveyor purity metrics and features real-time threshold sliders to dial in the AI's acceptance bounds. It also houses our Active Learning Review interface—operators can audit flagged Amber Queue images, re-label low-confidence items, and write updates back to the SQLite retraining database. On the left, we showcase the citizen-facing **Recycling Coach Portal**. Citizens log in securely via hashed phone numbers, upload single images of their recycling bags to receive instant separation coaching, and earn Eco-Credits when their bags meet high purity thresholds.*
> 
> *The direct impact of this integration is significant: facilities can save up to RM 250 per tonne in tipping fees by diverting waste, increase bale resale values by 20% to 35% through certified purity scores, and eliminate compactor fire liabilities by successfully catching batteries early."*

### Section 6: Conclusion (1.0 minute)
> *"In conclusion, manual sorting is a slow, hazardous, and unaudited process. PurityLoop AI bridges deep learning and business analytics, offering an automated, safe, and audited circular economy platform. By combining robust MLOps quality gates, asymmetric safety thresholds, and interactive recycler coaching, we protect facility operators and provide verifiable operational reporting data. Thank you for your time, and we now welcome any questions you may have."*

## 3. Panel Q&A Cheat Sheet (Anticipating Dr. Narishah's Questions)

### Q1: Why did you choose YOLOv8m-seg over larger models or standard bounding boxes?
*   **Answer:** *"Larger variants (YOLOv8l/x) exceed our Colab T4 16GB GPU compute limits, leading to out-of-memory errors during multi-dataset training, whereas the nano and small variants failed to reach our $\ge 0.80$ AP target on small, deformed objects like battery cells. YOLOv8m-seg provides the optimal balance of recall accuracy and low latency ($\le 500$ms on demonstration edge hardware). We choose instance segmentation polygons over standard bounding boxes because they allow us to calculate the actual pixel-area coverage of the item, which we calibrate to physical volume and mass—crucial for computing the material yield weights."*

### Q2: Why is the purity rate factor multiplied at the end of the purity rate formula?
*   **Answer:** *"Under international operational performance auditing standards like operational audit, environmental claims must reflect actual recycled outcomes. If a facility sorts a bale of aluminum that is 15% contaminated with organic residues, only 85% of that bale can be successfully smelted into recycled stock—the contaminated portion is discarded. Multiplying the maximum theoretical savings by the session purity rate scales down our ESG carbon claims to reflect what was actually recovered, ensuring a clean, audit-ready data trail that withstands external review."*

### Q3: What happens to a battery detection that falls in the "Amber" confidence range?
*   **Answer:** *"If a battery is detected with confidence between 45% and 89%, the system sounds a visual and auditory alert and holds the item in the local database's active learning queue. The physical conveyor belt routes this batch to a manual QA siding. The operator reviews the crop on the Streamlit dashboard, confirms the classification, and inputs the correct label. Once verified, the battery is safely quarantined, and the confirmed label is saved back to the database as a high-value training sample for the next model retraining loop."*
